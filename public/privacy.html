Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 19, 2026

OPRealm is designed for young creators, so privacy and child safety are treated as core parts of the product.

Child privacyParent visibilitySafety logs

This is a starter policy for launch preparation. It should be reviewed by a qualified legal professional before paid public launch, especially because OPRealm is intended for children and teens.

Information We Collect

We may collect information needed to operate OPRealm, provide creation path access, support parents, review public submissions, and keep creator tools safe.

  • Parent or guardian contact details, such as name and email address.
  • Student account details needed for learning access, such as display name, age band, creation path, progress, certificates, and project submissions.
  • Account and membership details used for access, safety gating, review workflows, and AI tool credits.
  • Learning activity, such as lesson completion, quiz status, badges, and project milestones.
  • AI tool usage, such as tool type, Creator credits used, prompt previews, and estimated generation cost.
  • Technical information, such as device, browser, approximate location, pages visited, and security logs.

How We Use Information

  • To provide access to creation paths, certificates, AI tools, reviewed library features, and reusable asset systems.
  • To help parents understand student progress and safety completion.
  • To protect students through moderation, safety checks, and access controls.
  • To respond to support requests and improve creation path quality.
  • To prevent abuse, bullying, scams, unsafe sharing, or misuse of AI tools.
  • To process payments, refunds, and account changes when paid memberships are active.

Children's Privacy

OPRealm is built for children and teens, but parent or guardian involvement is required. Students should not share passwords, addresses, phone numbers, school names, social handles, or private contact details inside OPRealm.

Where required by law, OPRealm will seek appropriate parent or guardian consent before collecting personal information from children.

AI Tool Data

Students may use Creator Credits to generate ideas, images, storyboards, sprite sheets, sounds, music, songs, or other creative assets. Prompts must be child-appropriate and must not include personal information. AI activity may be logged for safety, support, abuse prevention, and cost tracking.

Sharing Information

We do not sell student personal information. We may share limited information with service providers that help operate OPRealm, such as hosting, payments, analytics, email, safety review, AI generation, and learning platforms.

Data Security

We use reasonable safeguards including access controls, safety gating, private moderation logs, and secure hosting. No online service can guarantee complete security, so students are taught to keep personal information private.

Parent Rights

Parents or guardians may contact OPRealm to ask about student information, request corrections, request deletion where appropriate, or raise privacy concerns.

Contact

For privacy questions, contact: support@oprealm.com

Safety-first access

Students complete safety training before public publishing and broader AI tool access.

Parent transparency

Policies are written to help parents understand how OPRealm protects young creators.

Private by default

AI outputs and safety reports are designed to be reviewed privately first.
