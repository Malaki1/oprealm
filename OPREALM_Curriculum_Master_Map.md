# OPREALM Curriculum Master Map

## Course Vision

OPREALM is a safety-first AI game creation academy for kids. Students learn how to build games through guided projects while developing online safety habits, digital confidence, creative problem solving, and responsible AI use.

## MVP Launch Scope

The first launch should focus on the beginner pathway only:

1. Safety Academy
2. Tech Setup & Readiness
3. Roblox Studio Foundations
4. First Obby Builder
5. Beginner Certification

This gives families a complete, understandable outcome: the child learns how to stay safe online, gets Roblox Studio set up, learns the basics, and finishes a playable first game.

## Final MVP Outcome

By the end of the beginner pathway, students can safely use Roblox Studio, navigate the workspace, build a simple obby, add basic gameplay elements, test their game, and explain the online safety rules required before sharing or playing multiplayer games.

## Learning Principles

- Safety comes before creation.
- Every lesson should create a small visible win.
- Every module should end with a quiz, task, or project submission.
- Videos should be short, visual, and beginner friendly.
- The AI Coach should guide, explain, and hint without doing the whole task.
- Parents should always be able to see progress, safety completion, certificates, and project outcomes.

## Module 1: Safety Academy

### Goal

Students understand the core online safety rules for gaming, multiplayer worlds, chat, AI tools, and publishing projects.

### Lessons

| Lesson | Objective | Activity | Assessment | Assets Needed | AI Coach Role |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Welcome To OPREALM Safety | Student understands why safety comes first. | Watch intro and choose safety pledge. | Safety pledge completion. | Facecam intro, mascot animation. | Explain why safety matters in kid-friendly language. |
| Private Information | Student can identify information they should never share online. | Sort safe vs private information cards. | Scenario quiz. | Animated explainer, quiz cards. | Give hints during scenarios. |
| Safe Chat Behaviour | Student can describe respectful and safe chat habits. | Choose safe responses to chat examples. | Chat safety quiz. | Scenario slides, Roblox-style chat examples. | Explain why a message is safe or unsafe. |
| Scams And Fake Rewards | Student can spot common fake currency, item, and link scams. | Identify scam examples. | Scam spotting quiz. | Animated scam examples. | Warn against suspicious links or free reward promises. |
| Reporting And Blocking | Student knows how to report, block, and ask for help. | Watch Roblox reporting demo. | Screenshot or quiz task. | Screen recording. | Remind student to involve a trusted adult. |
| Multiplayer Safety | Student understands public servers, private servers, friend lists, and voice chat risks. | Complete multiplayer safety checklist. | Checklist pass. | Visual checklist. | Explain public vs private multiplayer. |
| Healthy Gaming Habits | Student understands breaks, posture, frustration, and screen balance. | Create a healthy gaming plan. | Short reflection task. | Parent handout. | Encourage calm breaks when stuck. |
| Safety Final Check | Student proves safety readiness before unlocking build lessons. | Complete final safety quiz. | Required pass mark. | Final quiz. | Give hints, not direct answers. |

### Module Reward

Certificate: Safe Online Creator

## Module 2: Tech Setup & Readiness

### Goal

Students and parents can prepare the child for lessons, including account setup, installation, screenshots, file navigation, audio checks, and basic troubleshooting.

### Lessons

| Lesson | Objective | Activity | Assessment | Assets Needed | AI Coach Role |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Parent Setup Guide | Parent understands what the child needs before starting. | Parent watches setup overview. | Parent confirmation. | Parent video, checklist PDF. | Answer parent setup questions. |
| Creating A Roblox Account | Student and parent understand safe account setup. | Follow account setup guide. | Parent confirms setup. | Screen recording, privacy checklist. | Remind not to share passwords. |
| Installing Roblox Studio | Student can install and open Roblox Studio. | Install or verify Studio opens. | Screenshot upload. | Installation guide. | Troubleshoot common install issues. |
| Computer Basics | Student understands folders, saving, copy/paste, and downloads. | Save a practice file in a folder. | Screenshot upload. | Practice worksheet. | Explain steps slowly. |
| Screenshots And Help Requests | Student can take a screenshot and explain what went wrong. | Take a screenshot and write one help sentence. | Upload screenshot. | Screenshot guide. | Help write clear support requests. |
| Keyboard, Mouse, And Trackpad Basics | Student understands basic controls for learning. | Complete input checklist. | Checklist completion. | Keyboard overlay, trackpad guide. | Adapt help based on device. |
| Audio And Microphone Check | Student can check sound and microphone if needed. | Run audio check. | Parent/student confirmation. | Audio check guide. | Troubleshoot sound basics. |
| Troubleshooting Basics | Student learns restart, update, screenshot, and ask for help flow. | Practice troubleshooting scenario. | Short quiz. | Troubleshooting flowchart. | Guide student through safe steps. |

### Module Reward

Badge: Ready To Build

## Module 3: Roblox Studio Foundations

### Goal

Students can navigate Roblox Studio, use core building tools, test their work, and organize simple objects.

### Lessons

| Lesson | Objective | Activity | Assessment | Assets Needed | AI Coach Role |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Opening Roblox Studio | Student can open Studio and identify the main panels. | Label interface areas. | Mini quiz. | Screen recording, interface map. | Explain Explorer, Properties, Viewport. |
| Camera Controls | Student can orbit, pan, zoom, and move around the workspace. | Complete camera control challenge. | Reach all markers in practice map. | Practice map, keyboard overlay. | Give device-specific control tips. |
| Selecting And Moving Objects | Student can select, move, and duplicate parts. | Move blocks into target zones. | Screenshot task. | Practice map. | Explain selection mistakes. |
| Scaling And Rotating Parts | Student can resize and rotate objects accurately. | Match block shapes to outlines. | Screenshot task. | Challenge template. | Explain handles and rotation. |
| Parts, Materials, And Colours | Student can create basic parts and customize their look. | Build a themed platform room. | Project screenshot. | Materials demo file. | Suggest safe, readable design choices. |
| Grouping And Organizing | Student can group objects and name them clearly. | Group a mini obstacle set. | Checklist task. | Organization guide. | Recommend simple names. |
| Spawn Points And Play Testing | Student can add a spawn point and test their game. | Add spawn and press play. | Short video or screenshot. | Screen recording. | Help with test mode confusion. |
| Foundation Challenge | Student combines basic tools into a small playable room. | Build a mini obstacle room. | Submission review. | Challenge brief. | Checklist mode only. |

### Module Reward

Badge: Roblox Studio Explorer

## Module 4: First Obby Builder

### Goal

Students build a simple playable obby with hazards, checkpoints, coins, a win zone, and safe publishing preparation.

### Lessons

| Lesson | Objective | Activity | Assessment | Assets Needed | AI Coach Role |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| What Makes A Good Obby? | Student understands start, challenge, progress, and finish. | Review simple obby examples. | Design quiz. | Example gameplay clips. | Suggest beginner-friendly layouts. |
| Building The Start Area | Student creates a clear start point and first platform. | Build start zone. | Screenshot task. | Starter template. | Help with scale and spacing. |
| Jump Platforms | Student creates fair jump sections. | Build 5 jumps. | Playtest pass. | Jump distance guide. | Help adjust difficulty. |
| Lava And Hazards | Student adds a safe beginner hazard. | Add lava kill part. | Script check or playtest. | Script snippet, demo video. | Explain hazard script line by line. |
| Checkpoints | Student adds checkpoints so progress is saved during play. | Add 2 checkpoints. | Playtest pass. | Checkpoint script/template. | Debug checkpoint issues. |
| Coins And Score | Student adds collectible coins and a simple score. | Add 5 coins. | Score increases during test. | Coin model, script snippet. | Explain events and variables simply. |
| Sounds And Feedback | Student adds simple sound effects and visual feedback. | Add jump/win/coin sounds. | Playtest check. | Audio asset list. | Suggest safe sound choices. |
| Simple UI And Win Zone | Student adds a win message or finish screen. | Build finish zone. | Screenshot/video task. | UI template. | Explain UI elements. |
| Safety Before Sharing | Student checks privacy, game name, description, and sharing rules. | Complete publishing safety checklist. | Required checklist pass. | Parent checklist. | Enforce safety rules. |
| Final Obby Submission | Student submits their finished first game. | Upload screenshots/video and reflection. | Final project review. | Project rubric. | Checklist mode only, no direct completion. |

### Module Reward

Certificate: Junior Game Creator

OPREALM Beginner Creator Certificate

## AI Coach Safety Rules

The AI Coach should:

- never ask for passwords or personal information
- never encourage private chats with strangers
- never help bypass safety settings
- never give direct assessment answers
- always use age-appropriate language
- ask students to involve a parent for account, privacy, payment, or unsafe-contact issues
- offer hints, explanations, and debugging steps
- keep students encouraged when they are stuck
